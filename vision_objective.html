Formation

In 1904, Cooperative Agriculture Credit Act was enacted with the objective to insure credit to the farmers on easy terms through cooperative by starting cooperative movement in rural area of India in official manner which was first step towards cooperative. In the beginning, only two types of societies were organised - rural cooperative and urban cooperative. After the enactment of this act, different state governments enthusiastically implemented the provisions of this act and appointed Registrar and started effective educational program regarding cooperative which clearly reflected the progress in cooperative movement in next years. Few shortcomings were also noticed during the implementation which were removed, the area of operation was increased and a new Cooperative Act was brought in the year 1912. In this act, the difference between rural and urban cooperative societies was eliminated. Due to sufficient protection to extension of movement, it became possible to start organising cooperative societies for other purposes also besides providing agriculture credit to the farmers. After that, keeping in view the all-round development of cooperative movement, a new Cooperative Act was enforced in 1965.By exercising the power under section-130 of the act, the state government notified U.P. Cooperative Societies Rules 1968. At present, all the cooperative societies are discharging their function under these act and rules.

Vision/Objective

The vision of the organisation of Cooperative Department is not only provide cheaper credit to the farmers but improve the living standard of weaker and poor rural and urban people by making them prosperous. To achieve this objective, the department is providing financial assistance to cooperative societies by implementing different programs such as sale-purchase, cold storage, labour, consumer, cooperative credit and banking etc. The department works as a friend, philosopher and guide to cooperative societies issue necessary instruction to discharge their function and supervise them. Besides providing cheaper credit to the farmers and providing loan to poor for investment in the form of share in the society, the cooperative society also helps in processing and storage of goods and providing assistance in getting remunerative price of their produce by making arrangement of sale-purchase. The society also provide different farm inputs used in agriculture to the farmer at fair prices. It also distributes consumer goods through societies in rural/urban area under public distribution system. To make the primary agriculture cooperative societies multipurpose, which are at present 7479, there is arrangement of a shop and office at each society. In some societies, residential facility is also available for Secretary. To enhance the administrative capability of the staff working in cooperative society, the department is also arranging training for cooperative supervisors, secretaries working in primary agriculture credit societies and secretaries of District Cooperative Banks. To save the potato growers/traders from the exploitation of middlemen, Uttar Pradesh Potato Development Marketing Federation has been organised by the department. The population of scheduled caste/ scheduled tribes is 17% of the total population in the state. They do not have enough resources that they can become member of the cooperative credit organisations and strengthen they economic standard by availing economic assistance from the society. For social upliftment of this class, the department is proving short term loan/grant by bringing them under fold under Special Component Plan. The salary and wages of all the employees working in Cooperative Department is paid through department grant head of account-18 Agriculture and Allied other departments (Cooperative) and assistance for plans under Special Component Plan is received from the budget of Social Welfare Department grant head of account-83. Cooperative Services Selection Board was constituted in 1972 for selection of staff, administrative control and approval of their service rules. For quick enquiry and disposal of matters related to financial embezzlement, Special Investigation Branch, constituted in the department, investigates the cases on priority and severe action is taken against the person found guilty. Audit of accounts of cooperative societies and direction for its maintenance is issued by Chief Audit Officers, Cooperative Societies and Panchayats, Uttar Pradesh They inform serious issues of misappropriation of funds and embezzlements to Registrar and Office bearer of the society for necessary action. U.P. Cooperative Tribunal has also been constituted in 1971 in the state under cooperative sector.
