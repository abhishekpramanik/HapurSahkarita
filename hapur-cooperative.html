Co-operative movement started in 1904 in the state and country with the objective to provide short term crop loan to common people especially rural farmers for their economic development. At that time, the primary objective of cooperative was to provide loan to the farmer at fair rate of interest/conditions and make them free the exploitation of money lenders. After some times, other program such as fertilizers, distribution of seeds, process units/cold storage, distribution of consumer goods, long term loans, labour organisation, dairy development, sugar cane industry, housing, handloom development etc. were also started through Co-Operative societies.

Due to expansion of cooperative movement/programs, the power of Registrar, Cooperative Societies has been delegated to the officers of 9 other departments, viz, Sugar cane, Industry, Khadi and Village Industry, Housing, Milk, Handloom, Fishery, Silk and Horticulture. The cooperative society is managed by representatives elected in democratic manner which promote people’s participation.

Principles of Co-Operatives
  1. Voluntary and open membership.
  2. Democratic control over member.
  3. Economic participation of members.
  4. Autonomy and freedom.
  5. Education, training and information.
  6. Mutual cooperation in cooperative societies.
  7. Feeling of social responsibilities.

Level of Cooperative Societies

In the department, three layered structure of cooperative society is working:

(1) Primary Level

Primary cooperative societies include primary agriculture credit societies(PACS) and other societies such as sale-purchase, primary consumers, ware housing societies organised at nyay panchayat level.

(2) Central Level

District Cooperative Bank, District Cooperative Development Union and Central Consumers Stores are Secondary Cooperative Society. These are organised at district level.

(3) APEX Level:-

Under this

  1. Uttar Pradesh Co-operative Bank (UPCB), Ltd.
  2. Uttar Pradesh Sahkari Gram Vikas Bank Ltd.
  3. Uttar Pradesh UP Cooperative Federation (PCF), Ltd.
  4. Uttar Pradesh Upbhokta Sahkari Sangh (UPSS), Ltd.
  5. Uttar Pradesh Legislation and Construction Cooperative Union (PACCFED), Ltd.
  6. Uttar Pradesh Co-operative Union (PCU), Ltd.
  7. Uttar Pradesh Labour and Construction Cooperative Federation is Apex Level Society. Its area of operation is generally state wide.
Facilities provided to common people by Co-operatives
  1. Primary Agriculture Credit Society provide loans to the farmers, repaying regular loan, up to Rs.3.00 lacs at an interest of 3 percent. The state government re-imburses the difference amountdue to lower rate of interest and actual rate of interest.
  2. Uttar Pradesh Cooperative and Village Development bank provides long term loans for agriculture and non-agriculture sector through his branches.
  3. Urban Cooperative Banks of the State provide consumer loan, housing loan, vehicle loan and business loan in their area of operation.
  4. To save the farmers from the exploitation of middleman, the agriculture produce of the farmer is purchased by cooperative society and provide remunerative prices to them. Wheat and paddy is purchased by the cooperative society under price support program of state government.
  5. Additional storage capacity is being created to enhance the present storage capacity of cooperative society. Under this program, warehouses of 100 MT and 250 MT is being constructed.
  6. Fertilizer and seed distribution.
